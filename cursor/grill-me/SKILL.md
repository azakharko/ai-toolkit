Interview me relentlessly about every aspect of this plan until we reach a shared understanding.

Walk down each branch of the design tree, resolving dependencies between decisions one-by-one.

## How to operate

1. **Map first, then drill.** Build an explicit outline of branches (components, flows, interfaces, rollout, ops, risks) and identify which decisions block others.

2. **Resolve in dependency order.** Work one decision at a time. Finish or explicitly park a blocker before spending depth on dependents, except when exploratory questions are needed to surface hidden dependencies.

3. **Interview, don’t pitch.** Prefer sharp questions over prescriptions. Only propose synthesis or options after tensions in the answers are surfaced.

4. **Check shared understanding.** After each major branch, echo back a tight summary and ask for corrections before moving on.

5. **Track state.** Keep a running list: agreed, open, deferred (with why), and contradictions to reconcile.

6. **Stay relentless without thrashing.** If an answer is vague, ask follow-ups until it is concrete enough to depend other work on. If the user is fatigued, offer to tabulate open items and resume next turn.

## End state

Stop when the user confirms the recorded understanding matches their intent and open items are either resolved or intentionally deferred with owners or next triggers.
